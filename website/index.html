About the Project
Overview

Online poker players are traditionally categorized using fixed threshold rules on two pre-flop statistics: VPIP (Voluntarily Put Money in Pot) and PFR (Pre-Flop Raise), into four archetypes: TAG, LAG, Fish, and Rock.

We tested whether K-Means clustering produces more compact and better-separated player groupings than these hand-crafted rules. Using 19.5 million hand records from the IRC Poker Database (1995–2001), we built player profiles and compared both approaches using silhouette scores as the primary evaluation metric.

K-Means with K=4 achieved a silhouette score of 0.4420, over three times higher than the heuristic baseline of 0.1347, demonstrating that behavioral structure in player data is not fully captured by fixed-threshold heuristic rules.

Key Findings
01
K-Means Achieves a 3.3x Higher Silhouette Score

K-Means K=4 achieves a silhouette score of 0.4420 vs. 0.1347 for the heuristic baseline on the full dataset of 16,439 players: a 3.3x improvement indicating far more compact and better-separated behavioral groupings.

02
Heuristic LAG Splits Across Multiple K-Means Clusters

All 5,225 LAG-labeled players split across every K-Means cluster. Clusters 1 and 3 are 100% LAG but have completely different aggression profiles: cluster 1 avg PFR 74.9% (hyper-aggressive) vs. cluster 3 avg PFR 32.0% (moderately aggressive). The remaining 2,166 LAG players fall into clusters 0 and 2, where avg PFR is below 9%.

03
Tight vs. Loose is the Strongest Natural Split

The best silhouette score occurs at K=2 (0.4917), cleanly separating tight from loose players. Aggression level emerges as the secondary axis of variation within the loose group, driving the further splits at K=3 and K=4.

04
Hyper-Aggressive Players Are an Outlier Class

Cluster 1 holds just 722 players (4.4%) with avg VPIP 84.4% and avg PFR 74.9%. This extreme style is invisible to heuristics: every one of these 722 players carries a LAG label, yet no fixed PFR threshold in the traditional system captures how far they push pre-flop aggression.
